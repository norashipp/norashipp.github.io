Research

Discovery & Modeling of Milky Way Stellar Streams

Discovery of Stellar Streams in the Dark Energy Survey

I led the search for new stellar streams in the Dark Energy Survey (DES). We discovered 11 new streams and 4 previously known streams, increasing the population of known streams at the time by almost 50% and more than doubling the number of known streams in the Southern Hemisphere. To learn more about the DES streams check out the link below.

Discovery of Tidal Tails around the Globular Cluster Palomar 13

I discovered extended tidal tails around the globular cluster Palomar 13 (Pal 13) in the Legacy Surveys data. This is one of the few streams with a known projenitor, and conversely, one of the few globular clusters with known thin tidal tails.

  • Details

Measurement of the Proper Motions of the DES Streams with Gaia DR2

I measured the proper motions of the DES stream in Gaia DR2. This was made possible by cross-matching the precise DES photometry with the unprecedented astrometric measurements from Gaia. I was able to measure proper motions for even the most distance DES streams, out to 50 kpc.

The Southern Stellar Stream Spectroscopic Survey

The Southern Stellar Stream Spectroscopic Survey (S5) is an unprecedented systematic spectroscopic survey of stellar streams in the Southern Hemisphere. S5 aims to measure radial velocities and metallicities of streams across the southern sky. As the S5 proper motion coordinator and builder, I have assisted in target selection, proper motion measurements, stream detection, and modeling in several collaboration analyses. To learn more about our collaboration, please check out the website at the link below!

Weighing the Large Magellanic Cloud

The Large Magellanic Cloud (LMC) is the most massive satellite of the Milky Way and has a huge effect on many aspects of our Galaxy. However, its mass is still relatively unknown. Stellar streams are uniquely able to constrain the toal mass of the LMC. I have modeled six of the S5 streams to measure the mass of the LMC to ~1011 Mâ˜‰, and have established a consistent picture of how the Milky Way's largest perturber interacts with stellar streams in the southern sky.

The Population of S5 Streams

With data from DES, Gaia, and S5 we have assembled the first population of systematically-observed 6D stellar streams. Now, we can begin to study these streams on the popultion level and to make comparisons to the undisrupted satellites of our Galaxy in order to study the accretion history of the Milky Way.

Inclusion, Diversity, and Equity in Astronomy (IDEA)

I am the co-founder of the graduate student and postdoc-led group IDEA at the University of Chicago Department of Astronomy & Astrophysics and KICP. We seek to create a safe space for members of our community, educate ourselves through readings and discusison, and work to make changes in our department to make our community more accessible to all. Some of our achievements include founding a department mentoring program, establishing relocation funds for incoming grad students, and publishing the Petition to Support Black Astronomers.

Astrobites

Astrobites is a grad student-led collaboration that seeks to make astronomy journal articles more accessible. City of Tomorrow

I organized and led a summer institute where Chicago high schoolers designed civilizations of the future - on moons, space ships, and exoplanets.
